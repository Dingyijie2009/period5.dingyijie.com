Career Overview

Game developers turn ideas into playable games. They take on roles such as programmers, artists, designers, and testers, and often use game engines like Unity or Unreal. The work combines creativity, technical skills, and teamwork to make games people enjoy.

What they do daily

  • Meetings: Short team check-ins to plan and coordinate work.
  • Build & Fix: Writing code, making features, and fixing bugs.
  • Playtesting: Trying the game to find issues and improve the fun.
  • Documentation: Writing short notes or updates so others can follow changes.
  • Learning: Trying new tools or reading about trends to improve skills.

Career Progression

Entry-level roles

  • QA Tester: Test games and report bugs.
  • Community Support: Help players and share feedback with the team.
  • Junior Programmer/Artist/Designer: Assist the development team with basic tasks.
  • Technical Support: Troubleshoot hardware or software issues.

Skills & Experience

  • A portfolio of small projects or demos helps more than grades alone.
  • Basic programming (C#, C++, Python) is useful for many developer roles.
  • Familiarity with Unity or Unreal is a plus for development jobs.
  • Teamwork, communication, and problem-solving are important in every role.

Education & Training

Many employers value a bachelor's degree, but online courses, bootcamps, and internships are valid ways to gain experience.

Certifications

  • Unity or engine-specific certifications
  • 3D tool certifications (Autodesk Maya, 3ds Max) for artists
  • Agile/Scrum certificates for production and management roles

Salary & Job Outlook

Salary

Entry $40k–$60k CAD · Mid $60k–$90k CAD · Senior $90k+

Salaries vary by location, experience, and company size. Major studios and tech hubs typically pay more than small indie teams.

Job Outlook

The industry is growing but competitive. Areas like mobile, VR/AR, and AI are expanding, so keeping technical skills up to date helps with hiring.

  • Growth in mobile and immersive tech (VR/AR).
  • Strong demand for technical skills (programming, tools).
  • Practical experience and a good portfolio improve hiring chances.
